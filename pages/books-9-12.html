TIPS READS TOYS STORES

Kids age 9-12 are able to read both longer and more difficult books. They crave independence, so hand them this list of book recommendations and let them decide what to read next!

Harry Potter Series

J.K. Rowling

This bestselling series is one for the ages.

Ghosts

Raina Telgemeier

A graphic novel with more serious themes and realistic characters.

Hatchet

Gary Paulsen

A fascinating story of survival.

Wonder

R.J. Palacio

A community struggles with empathy, compassion and acceptance.

A Wrinkle in Time

Madeleine L'Engle

An exciting story of adventures in space and time.

Esperana Rising

Pan Munoz Ryan

The well-crafted story of a girl and her family in Mexico.

Harriet the Spy

Louise Fitzhugh

Coming-of-age tale of a lovable heroine.

Where the Red Fern Grows

Wilson Rawls

A story of love and adventure featuring a boy and his two dogs.

Holes

Louis Sachar

Humorous and interesting characters and a unique plot keep this story moving.

Bridge to Terabithia

Katherine Paterson

An enduring classic about growing up.

Number the Stars

Lois Lowry

A must-read about heroism in the face of terror and war.

Tales of a Fourth Grade Nothing

Judy Blume

Hilarious tales recounted from a kid's point of view.
